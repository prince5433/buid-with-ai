STUDY OVERVIEW

NeuraClear (NX-4821) is a monoclonal antibody targeting amyloid-beta oligomers for the treatment of moderate-to-severe Alzheimer's disease. This Phase III, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial (CLARITY-III) evaluated the efficacy and safety of NX-4821 in patients aged 55-85 years with confirmed Alzheimer's pathology.

Study Duration: 76 weeks (18-month treatment period + 4-week follow-up)
Enrollment Period: March 2023 — September 2024
Study Sites: 187 centers across 14 countries
Total Patients Enrolled: 2,847
Randomization: 1:1:1 (Low dose : High dose : Placebo)

PRIMARY EFFICACY RESULTS

Primary Endpoint: Change from baseline in ADAS-Cog14 score at Week 76

| Treatment Group | Baseline Mean | Week 76 Change | Difference vs Placebo | p-value    |
|----------------|---------------|----------------|----------------------|------------|
| Placebo         | 38.4          | +8.72          | —                    | —          |
| Low Dose (5mg)  | 38.1          | +5.84          | -2.88                | p < 0.001  |
| High Dose (10mg)| 38.6          | +4.21          | -4.51                | p < 0.0001 |

The high-dose group demonstrated a 51.7% reduction in cognitive decline compared to placebo, meeting the primary endpoint with high statistical significance.

ARIA (Amyloid-Related Imaging Abnormalities):
- Most ARIA events were mild-to-moderate and asymptomatic
- ARIA-E was detected primarily in the first 12 weeks of treatment
- 87% of ARIA-E cases resolved within 12 weeks
- 3 ARIA-E cases required hospitalization (all in high-dose group)
- ApoE4 homozygotes had higher ARIA-E rates (41.2% vs 24.8% in heterozygotes)

CONCLUSIONS

1. NeuraClear (NX-4821) at the 10mg dose demonstrated statistically significant and clinically meaningful slowing of cognitive and functional decline in patients with moderate-to-severe Alzheimer's disease.

2. The treatment effect of 51.7% reduction in cognitive decline represents a meaningful advance over currently available treatments.

3. Safety profile is consistent with the anti-amyloid antibody class, with ARIA being the most notable adverse effect. Enhanced MRI monitoring is recommended, particularly for ApoE4 carriers.

4. Biomarker data support the mechanism of action and suggest potential disease-modifying effects.

5. NovaNeuroscience plans to submit a Biologics License Application (BLA) to the FDA in Q2 2026 and a Marketing Authorization Application (MAA) to the EMA simultaneously.
