2. CEO REPORT — Q4 PERFORMANCE & STRATEGIC UPDATE

Robert Harrison presented the Q4 2025 performance summary:

Key Highlights:
- Total revenue: $234.7M (exceeding guidance of $210-225M)
- Three new solar farm projects commenced in Texas, Nevada, and Arizona
- Partnership agreement signed with Pacific Gas & Electric (PG&E) for 500MW capacity
- Employee count reached 2,847 (up 34% YoY)
- Named to Fortune's "Fastest Growing Energy Companies" list

Strategic Update:
- The acquisition of WindTech Solutions (announced October 2025) is expected to close by February 2026, pending regulatory approval from the Federal Energy Regulatory Commission (FERC)
- International expansion plans progressing: European office in Hamburg, Germany scheduled to open Q2 2026
- New battery storage division launched, with first commercial installation planned for Q1 2026

DISCUSSION: 
Jennifer Adams raised concerns about the pace of international expansion given current exchange rate volatility. CFO Torres noted that the company has implemented currency hedging strategies covering 80% of projected European expenses through 2026.

Dr. Hassan asked about the competitive landscape in battery storage. CTO Nguyen reported that GreenFuture's proprietary solid-state battery technology provides a 2-3 year competitive advantage based on current patent filings.

ACTION ITEM: Michael Torres to prepare a detailed currency risk analysis for the next board meeting.
DEADLINE: March 15, 2026
RESPONSIBLE: Michael Torres, CFO

Capital Expenditure:
- Q4 CapEx: $87.4M (primarily solar farm construction)

DISCUSSION:
Samantha Wright asked about the significant increase in proposed CapEx for 2026. Torres explained that $150M is earmarked for the battery storage division, $180M for new solar installations, and $95-145M for wind farm expansion following the WindTech acquisition.

Patricia Lawson requested confirmation that all project financing arrangements comply with new IRS clean energy tax credit requirements under the Inflation Reduction Act. Torres confirmed compliance and noted that the company expects to realize approximately $45M in tax credits for FY2025.

MOTION: David Kim moved to approve the 2026 CapEx budget range of $425-475M. Seconded by Dr. Hassan.
VOTE: Approved (7-1, Jennifer Adams abstaining pending currency risk review).

4. CTO REPORT — TECHNOLOGY & R&D PIPELINE

Dr. Lisa Nguyen presented the technology update:

R&D Expenditure: $28.4M in Q4 (12.1% of revenue)

Key Technology Developments:
1. Solid-State Battery Breakthrough: Lab prototype achieving 450 Wh/kg energy density (vs. industry standard 250 Wh/kg). Expected timeline to commercial production: 18-24 months.

2. AI-Powered Grid Management: Proprietary GAIA (Grid AI Analytics) platform now managing 3.2 GW of renewable capacity across 47 installations. Reduces curtailment by an estimated 23% and increases energy capture by 8.4%.

3. Next-Gen Solar Panel Design: New perovskite-silicon tandem cell achieving 31.2% efficiency in lab conditions (previous record: 29.8%). Patent filed November 2025.

4. Predictive Maintenance System: ML-based system deployed across all wind turbines, reducing unplanned downtime by 42% and maintenance costs by $12.3M annually.

Patent Portfolio:
- Total active patents: 187
- Patents filed in Q4: 12
- Pending applications: 34

DISCUSSION:
Dr. Hassan commended the battery technology progress and asked about manufacturing partnerships. Dr. Nguyen indicated preliminary discussions with two major battery manufacturers in South Korea and Japan, with an MOU expected by Q2 2026.

ACTION ITEM: Dr. Nguyen to present a detailed battery commercialization roadmap at the next board meeting.
DEADLINE: March 2026 Board Meeting
RESPONSIBLE: Dr. Lisa Nguyen, CTO
