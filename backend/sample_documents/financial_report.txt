EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Acme Technologies Inc. reports strong fourth quarter results for fiscal year 2025, with total revenue reaching $847.3 million, representing a 23% year-over-year increase. Net income rose to $142.6 million, driven by expansion in our cloud services division and strategic cost optimization across the enterprise segment.

The Board of Directors has approved a quarterly dividend of $0.45 per share, payable on March 15, 2026, to shareholders of record as of February 28, 2026.

SEGMENT ANALYSIS

1. CLOUD SERVICES ($412.5M, +38.4% YoY)

Our cloud services segment continued its rapid expansion, driven by strong adoption of the AcmeCloud Enterprise platform. Key achievements include:

- Active enterprise customers grew to 4,847 (up from 3,612 in Q4 2024)
- Average revenue per user (ARPU) increased 12% to $85,100 annually
- Cloud infrastructure capacity expanded to 28 data centers globally
- New partnerships with Microsoft Azure and AWS marketplace integration
- Customer retention rate maintained at 97.3%

The launch of AcmeCloud AI Suite in October 2025 contributed $47.2M in incremental revenue, exceeding our initial forecast of $30M. This AI-powered analytics platform has been adopted by 834 enterprise clients within its first quarter.

2. ENTERPRISE SOFTWARE ($215.8M, +8.6% YoY)

Enterprise software revenue showed steady growth, supported by the transition to subscription-based licensing. Notable developments:

- Subscription revenue now represents 78% of segment total (up from 62%)
- AcmeERP 12.0 release drove 340 new enterprise deals
- Government sector contracts valued at $45.2M secured in Q4
- Integration partnerships with Salesforce and SAP expanded

3. PROFESSIONAL SERVICES ($118.2M, +12.1% YoY)

Professional services benefited from increased demand for cloud migration consulting:

- Average project value increased to $2.1M (up from $1.7M)
- Billable utilization rate improved to 82.4%
- New consulting practice focused on AI implementation launched
- Team expanded by 215 consultants to 1,847 total

4. HARDWARE SOLUTIONS ($100.8M, +16.8% YoY)

Hardware solutions exceeded expectations due to renewed enterprise infrastructure spending:

- Next-gen server appliances contributed $42.3M
- Edge computing devices showed 67% growth
- Supply chain improvements reduced lead times by 35%

OUTLOOK FOR FY2026

Management provides the following guidance for fiscal year 2026:

- Revenue: $3.6B - $3.8B (24-30% growth)
- Operating Margin: 29-31%
- Capital Expenditure: $280-320M
- Cloud Services growth target: 35-40%
- New product launches planned: 4 major releases
- Planned headcount additions: ~2,500

Key risks include macroeconomic uncertainty, competitive pressure from emerging AI startups, and potential regulatory changes in data privacy legislation across EU markets.
